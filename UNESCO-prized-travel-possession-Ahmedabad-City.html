The Glorius History - Bhadra Fort

Ahmed Shah set up Ahmedabad as the new capital of Gujarat Sultanate and constructed Bhadra Fort on the east bank of the Sabarmati stream. The region inside the fortress had gotten involved by metropolitan improvements by 1525. After being taken over for year by the mughal empire leaders, Sarsenapati Umabaisaheb Khanderao Dabhade turned into the main female Commander-in-Chief throughout the entire existence of the Marathas in 1732. She instructed the Maratha Army and battled a battle close to Ahmedabad at Bhadra Fort vanquishing Mughal Sardar Joravar Khan Babi.

 

During the First Anglo–Maratha War (1775–1782), General Thomas Wyndham Goddard with 6,000 soldiers raged Bhadra Fort and caught Ahmedabad on 15 February 1779. After the war, the fortification was later given back to Marathas under the Treaty of Salbai. Ahmedabad was vanquished by the British in 1817.The fortification complex was utilized as a jail during the British Raj. Azam Khan sarai at present houses the administration workplaces, an ASI office, a mailing station and the city's affable courts. It is likewise utilized for banner lifting on Independence Day and Republic Day.
