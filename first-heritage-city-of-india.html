UNESCO World Heritage Site - Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad has developed over the course of six centuries, going through intervals of cyclic decline and development. In general, the city's fabric and urbanity still embody unity and sanctity, and it has absorbed shifts and development with its usual resilience. With its plot that has persisted over decades, the old city is regarded as an archaeological body. Its metropolitan archaeology, based on the remains from the Pre-Sultanate and Sultanate eras, adds to its cultural value. A lot of factors culminated with these is why the heritage city of Ahmedabad is India’s first UNESCO World Heritage City.

The ancient city's timber-based architecture is of extraordinary importance and is the most distinctive feature of its heritage. It exemplifies Ahmadabad's significant contribution to local customs, arts and crafts, structure architecture and resource collection, and its ties with legends and symbols that stressed its stigmas associated with the inhabitants.

This blending of old and modern in Ahmedabad's Old City led to its designation as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2017. It's impossible not to be charmed by this juxtaposition as we walk along the cobbled streets that make up its innumerable neighborhoods, admiring the wonderfully ornate, exotic architectures of centuries-old suburban buildings.

Rich heritage has become a “go to” spot not only for travelers and afficionados, but also for entrepreneurs who want to start home and office accommodations for these lovers. Many archaeologists have been drawn to the notion of living in luxury residences with a backstory, with the interior capturing the spirit of the period.   Isn't there a historian among each of us? Our captivating characters would love visiting these locations and have a hands on experience, don't you think?

The Locations

Sabarmati Ashram



Just eight kilometers across the prime location of Shivalik Sharda Harmony, which is located in the university area, on the banks of Sabarmati river we have the historic and culturally rich- Sabarmati Ashram. Historically known as "Satyagraha Ashram", Sabarmati Ashram was located at the Kocharab Bungalow in Ahmedabad. It was only a few years later when it was shifted to the banks of river Sabarmati and renamed, which we now know as Sabarmati Ashram.

Jhulta Minara



A tour to UNESCO’S World Heritage Site i.e., India’s first heritage city- Ahmedabad's historical sites will be incomplete without a stop at Jhulta Minara or Shaking Minarets, an interesting architectural wonder. The two minarets were once the entrance to the 500-year-old Sidi Bashir Mosque. A very strategically chosen location of Shivalik Sharda Harmony is more centrally located in this web of historical locations. Jhulta Minara is merely being just a 20 minute ride from Shivalik Sharda Harmony which offers ‘Green Building’ features such as landscaped gardens so you and your family do not even have to worry about a healthy indoor environment.

Teen Darwaza



While you're here, don't forget to look at the manuscript on the gate's pillars of Teen Darwaza. Another significant historical feature in the city is Teen Darwaza, the eastern entrance to the Bhadra Fort. This entry portal is so well-known that it is used in the UNESCO heritage site- Ahmedabad’s Municipal Corporation's logo. Made by a Maratha governor in 1812, the inscription declares that women have the same right as men to inherit ancestral lands. Just a ten kilometer walk from Shivalik Sharda Harmony, which offers more to every age bracket of the people we have got a lush green lawn for senior citizens where they can relax, exercise and catch up with their group every single evening. For the fitness enthusiasts, a fully functional gym to keep their workout game on peak while being in the vicinity of the heritage area in the Ahmedabad Heritage City.
